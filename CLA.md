## Plain-language summary

**You keep your copyright.** This is a licence, not an assignment. You remain the owner of
everything you write and you can use, sell, or relicense your own work anywhere else, freely
and forever.

**What you give OrbitQube** is permission to license your contribution to other people,
including under licences that are not the AGPL. Stated directly, because it is the part worth
understanding before you sign: **OrbitQube can include your contribution in a paid commercial
edition of DeckTrail, and can do so without paying you.**

**What OrbitQube must do in return** is keep releasing your contribution under the AGPL-3.0.
That is a condition on the commercial right, not a promise of goodwill: see Section 2.3. If we
stop honouring it, the permission in Section 2 stops with it. This is what "in addition, never
instead" means, and it is why it is written into the operative text rather than only here.

**Why we ask.** DeckTrail is AGPL-3.0 and self-hosting stays free. Some organisations cannot
accept the AGPL and will pay for a commercial licence instead; that is the intended way this
project funds its own maintenance. That option only exists if a single party holds the rights
to license the whole codebase. Without this agreement, one contribution from one person
forecloses it permanently.

If you are not comfortable with this, please do not sign. Open an issue describing the change
instead; a maintainer can implement it independently, and the report is genuinely useful.

## How to sign

**Do not use a bare `Signed-off-by` trailer for this.** That trailer already means assent to the
[Developer Certificate of Origin](https://developercertificate.org/), which is a different
document that grants no licence at all. Using it here would mean quietly redefining a
well-known token so that people agree to a commercial relicensing grant while believing they
agreed to a provenance statement. We are not doing that.

Instead:

**1. Add your name to `CLA-SIGNATURES.md`** in your first merge request:

```
- Full Name <email@example.com>  signed DeckTrail CLA v1.0 on YYYY-MM-DD
```

**2. Add this trailer to each commit** in that merge request and every one after:

```
DeckTrail-CLA-1.0-signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>
```

The trailer names the document and its version, so it says what you agreed to and when. If a
later version of this CLA changes the terms, it gets a new version number and asks you again,
rather than silently applying new terms to an old signature.

If a contribution is merged without this, that is our mistake and not yours. We will ask you
afterwards. If you would rather not agree, we will remove the contribution: without your assent
no licence under Section 2 ever came into existence, so removal is the only honest remedy. Be
aware that we can remove it from the current code, but we cannot rewrite git history or recall
a published container image, so tell us sooner rather than later.
